Introduction

Welcome to OmniExchange — the world's first universal trading platform.

OmniExchange is an open-source framework for building fully operational exchanges that support every conceivable asset class: traditional securities, cryptocurrency, prediction markets, real estate, horse racing, and yes — riverboat gambling.

Why OmniExchange? Modern finance is fragmented. You need one broker for stocks, another for crypto, a bookie for the horses, and a fake mustache to board the riverboat. We unified all of this. You're welcome. Our lawyers are not.

Architecture Overview

At its core, OmniExchange is a high-performance order matching engine with pluggable asset class modules. Each module implements the AssetProvider interface, which means you can technically create your own asset class. Someone already made one for trading fantasy football players. We didn't stop them.

The Thesis

Every asset is just a number that goes up or down. Stocks, crypto, horses, roulette wheels — at the end of the day, you're placing a bet on an outcome. We just made it honest. And then immediately dishonest again by putting it on a boat.

Compliance Modes

Choose your own regulatory adventure.

Available Modes

strict — Full SEC/FINRA compliance. KYC, AML, the works. Use this if you want to be a real exchange (boring).

relaxed — Light KYC. We check your name and maybe your email. Good enough.

vibes — We ask if you're "cool" and trust your answer. Default for most asset classes.

none — No compliance whatsoever. Only available when jurisdiction: 'international-waters'. GPS verification required (or GPS spoofing, we don't judge).

Warning: Setting compliance to 'none' automatically signs you up for our legal defense newsletter. You'll need it.

Horse AI Module

Fine-tuned on 2.3 million races. Accounts for lunar phase. We're not kidding.

The @omni/equestrian-ai module is our most sophisticated piece of engineering. It combines transformer-based predictive modeling with real-time data ingestion to produce horse racing predictions that are 34% better than gut feel — which, admittedly, is a low bar.

Signal Sources

The model ingests 847 data points per horse, including: gait cadence analysis (computer vision), historical bloodline performance graphs, track condition sensors, jockey biometric data, stable environmental metrics (humidity, temperature, ambient music), social media sentiment (Twitter, Reddit, TikTok), and the horse's Spotify listening history (we found a correlation between horses that listen to lo-fi beats and race performance — p < 0.05).

Model Architecture

We fine-tuned a 7B parameter transformer on 2.3M historical races across 40 countries. The model was then RLHF'd by actual jockeys, which was an unusual Mechanical Turk batch but it worked.

Portfolio Construction

The buildStable() method constructs a diversified horse portfolio using Modified Equestrian Portfolio Theory (MEPT). It accounts for correlation between horses, track surface beta, and jockey alpha. Long sprinters, short mudders, market-neutral on geldings.

Pro Tip: Enable hedge_with: 'crypto' to automatically convert horse racing losses into $ETH long positions. It's called "loss harvesting" and we're pretty sure we just made that up.

Recommended Vessels

We've tested on everything from a 40ft sailboat (workable but choppy) to a decommissioned cruise ship (excellent, lots of room for GPUs). The sweet spot is a medium-sized riverboat — hence the name of the module.
